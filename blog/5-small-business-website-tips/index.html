5 Critical Aspects of Small Business Websites

Posted on June 4, 2018

Does your website follows these best practices?

Even if not directly, small businesses are always in competition with the biggest players in the industry. In order for small firms to survive, they need to capitalize on every opportunity in front of them. A well-designed, optimized, and functional website can give small businesses a leg-up on their competitors. As a small business owner, having a great website can help you stand out and thrive in an increasingly competitive business landscape. To create a great website for your business, the following five critical aspects must be incorporated:

1) Clear Top Navigation

If you’ve considered putting your website’s navigation bar on the side, understand that this may not be the most effective option for your business. Most users will expect the navigation bar to be placed on the top of your website, so placing it on the side could make things unnecessarily confusing.

2) Contact Information

As a small business, your contact information must be easily visible on your website. This information should show your business’s name, physical address, telephone number, and business email. It should be fashioned in an easily accessible location, such as on the footer menu or the top menu bar (preferably both).

3) Site Security

You want to give your visitors the confidence that your website is secure. Nobody should come to your website and see a “site unsafe” notification. This calls for securing your site with an SSL certificate and having the security signature shown in the URL. This is especially important if you will be collecting sensitive customer information such as credit card numbers.

4) Mobile Friendly

Mobile users have now surpassed desktop users, and as a small business, you must accommodate your customer base. It goes without saying that current websites must be mobile-friendly, and as such, it is imperative for you to test your site and make sure that it renders correctly on mobile devices. Failure to do this can result in the loss of potential customers before they even see what you have to offer.

5) Suitable Content Management System

As a small business, you may not have the resources to hire a development expert to take care of updating your website with fresh content on a regular basis. But luckily for you, there are a wide variety of affordable content management systems, like Vitris, that you can use even if you have no prior website design experience. Consider using a content management system to easily build and maintain your business’s website.
